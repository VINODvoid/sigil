## Business Model

### Revenue Streams

**1. Transaction Fees (Primary, High Volume)**
- 0.1% of transactions gated by Sigil verification
- Capped at $1 per transaction (low-friction)
- Projected: highest volume stream at scale

**2. Attestation Issuance (Enterprise)**
- Companies pay to issue Sigils to their agents
- Tiered: $10/month (10 agents) → $1K/month (unlimited)
- Similar to Okta's per-seat pricing
- KYC providers pay to become registered attesters

**3. Reputation API (Developer)**
- Metered queries against reputation data
- Free tier: 1K queries/month
- Paid: $0.001 per query
- Used by agent platforms doing vendor selection

**4. Registry Premium (Optional)**
- Boosted visibility in registry search
- Verified organization badge
- $50-500/month depending on tier

### Unit Economics (Projected)

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Cost per credential issuance | <$0.01 (Solana fees) |
| Revenue per active agent/month | $1-10 (variable) |
| Gross margin | 90%+ (software) |
| Customer acquisition cost | Low (dev-led growth) |
| Time to payback | <3 months |

## Moat / Defensibility

Two-sided network:
- **Agents want in** where verification is common (more trust = more transactions)
- **Principals want the standard** where agents already exist
- **Integrators want the leading protocol** (devs don't want to integrate 3 competitors)

Once 1,000+ Sigils exist and registry has X listings, switching cost is high.

### Brand Moat (Medium-Strong)

- First KYA protocol = thought leader position
- Accelerator backing = ecosystem blessing

### Data Moat (Compounds Over Time)

- Dispute resolution precedents accumulate

## Competitive Moat

### Why Incumbents Can't Just Build This

**Coinbase (x402 maker):**
- Focused on payment layer, not identity
- Commerce/exchange DNA, not identity/trust
- Building this would be a distraction from their core

**MoonPay (OWS maker):**
- Focused on wallet layer
- Would compete with their own infrastructure partners
- Their customers ARE Sigil's customers

**Big Cloud (AWS, GCP, Azure):**
- Won't build crypto-native
- Will rely on neutral protocols like Sigil

**OpenAI / Anthropic:**
- Platform players
- Will adopt neutral standards, not build competing ones

**Ethereum Identity Projects (Gitcoin Passport, Verax):**
- Human identity focus
- Ethereum-centric
- Don't solve agent-specific problems (spending limits, domain constraints)

### What WOULD Kill Sigil

1. **Moving too slow** — another team ships first, becomes standard
2. **Too protocol-focused, zero UX** — devs pick easier-to-integrate alternative
3. **Regulatory overreach** — agents get regulated like humans (KYA becomes proprietary)
4. **Solana collapses** — low probability, but existential
